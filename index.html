South Bronx resource center combats diaper need

In the South Bronx, the struggle to afford diapers reflects a national trend.

By Charlotte Isidore - December 1, 2025

Diaper inventory at The HopeLine in Longwood, Bronx. (Credit: Charlotte Isidore)

At The HopeLine, the Bronx’s first diaper distribution center, Executive Director Maria Cintron said demand has sharply increased.
National data show that about half of U.S. households with children aged 4 and under cannot afford enough diapers.

“The demand alone of just seeing people waiting to make an appointment has doubled,” Cintron said.

Human Resources Administration data shows the five community districts with the highest share of SNAP users are all in the Bronx.
In the district The HopeLine serves, 40% of residents relied on SNAP as of April 2024.

Though diapers are essential for children’s health, they are excluded from SNAP and WIC.

Cintron launched The HopeLine’s diaper program in 2015 after families began requesting them alongside food.
Today, the organization serves about 800 children a month and distributes roughly 35,000 diapers.
