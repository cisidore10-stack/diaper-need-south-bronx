South Bronx resource center combats diaper need

In the South Bronx, the struggle to afford diapers reflects a national trend.

By Charlotte Isidore - December 3, 2025

Maria Cintron speaks to clients outside of he HopeLine in Longwood, Bronx. (Credit: Charlotte Isidore)

At The HopeLine, the Bronx’s first diaper distribution center, Executive Director Maria Cintron said
demand has sharply increased. National data shows that about half of U.S. households with
children aged 4 and under cannot afford enough diapers.

“The demand alone of just seeing people waiting to make an appointment has doubled,” Cintron said.

NYC Human Resources Administration data from Sept. 2025 shows that 5 out of the 10
community districts with the highest number of SNAP recipients are in the Bronx.

Top 10 NYC community districts with the highest number of SNAP recipients (Human Resources Administration).

Though diapers are essential for children’s health, they are excluded from SNAP and WIC.

Cintron launched The HopeLine’s diaper program in 2015 after families began requesting them
alongside food. Today, the organization serves about 800 children a month and distributes
roughly 35,000 diapers.
